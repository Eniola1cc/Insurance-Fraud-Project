# Business Problem – Insurance Fraud Risk Prioritisation

## 1. Business Context

Insurance companies process a high volume of claims on a daily basis, many of which require manual investigation to detect potential fraud. However, investigative resources are limited, making it impractical to thoroughly review every claim.

As a result, fraudulent claims may go undetected, while investigators spend valuable time reviewing low-risk claims. This leads to financial losses, operational inefficiencies, and reduced effectiveness of fraud detection teams.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

There is currently no intelligent system to prioritise insurance claims based on their likelihood of being fraudulent.

Without a prioritisation mechanism:
- High-risk claims may not be reviewed in time  
- Investigators waste effort on low-risk claims  
- Fraud detection processes become inefficient and reactive  

---

## 3. Objective

The objective of this project is to develop a data-driven fraud risk prioritisation system that:

- Predicts the probability of fraud for each claim  
- Ranks claims based on risk level  
- Enables investigators to focus on the highest-risk claims first  

---

## 4. Business Impact

This system is designed to deliver measurable business value by:

- Increasing fraud detection rates  
- Reducing operational workload on investigators  
- Improving allocation of investigative resources  
- Minimising financial losses due to undetected fraud  

---

## 5. Key Business Questions

This project aims to answer the following:

1. Which claims should be prioritised for investigation?
2. How much fraud can be detected if only the top 10–20% of claims are reviewed?
3. What factors are most strongly associated with fraudulent claims?
4. How can fraud detection be improved without increasing operational costs?

---

## 6. Analytical Approach

To address this problem, the project will:

- Perform exploratory data analysis to identify fraud patterns  
- Engineer features that capture suspicious behaviour  
- Build predictive models to estimate fraud probability  
- Rank claims based on predicted fraud risk  

---

## 7. Success Criteria

The solution will be considered successful if it can:

- Accurately identify high-risk claims  
- Capture a significant proportion of fraud within a small percentage of reviewed claims  
- Provide interpretable insights into fraud drivers  

---

## 8. Scope

This project focuses on:
- Historical insurance claims data  
- Fraud detection at the claim level  

It does not cover:
- Real-time fraud detection systems  
- External data integration  

---

## 9. Conclusion

By transforming fraud detection from a manual, reactive process into a prioritised, data-driven system, this project aims to significantly improve efficiency and effectiveness in identifying fraudulent insurance claims.
